Discussion: Implications & Limitations

Implications for Cybersecurity

Human-Centric XAI is Key

The evaluation of XAI systems must shift from being purely model-centric to being human-centric. The ultimate test is not mathematical correctness, but utility for the human analyst.

XAI is a Tool, Not a Panacea

XAI frameworks like SHAP are powerful starting points, but they are not a "plug-and-play" solution. They require careful integration and post-processing to be effective in an operational workflow.

Limitations of This Study

Generalizability vs. Artifacts

While SHAP shows what the model learned, it's hard to distinguish between generalizable security rules and statistical artifacts specific to the static CIC-IDS-2017 dataset.

Lack of User Study

Our assessment of "utility" is based on established security principles. A formal user study with SOC analysts would be needed to definitively measure the impact on real-world workflows.
